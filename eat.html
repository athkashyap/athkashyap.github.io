Great restaurants to go in Los Angeles

Nakayama

Nakayama focuses her talent on kaiseki: a classical style of Japanese cooking that dictates a specific progression of textures, temperatures, tastes and seasonal ingredients. À la carte is not an option, and when every dish is this good, that’s okay by us. n/naka typically offers a 13-course tasting menu ($365) with a vegetarian option, plus wine and sake pairings. A newer renovation has given the dining room a sleeker look—with a requisite price bump to match—but n/naka still delivers the most delicious, wonderfully serene dining experience in the city.

Hayato

The space is intimate, the ceramics are handcrafted and imported from Japan, service is respectful and diligent, and chef-owner Brandon Go's precision and technique come by way of training in Japan for years.

Kato

After relocating to ROW DTLA in 2021, chef Jon Yao, front-of-house partner Nikki Reginaldo, sommelier Ryan Bailey and bar director Austin Hennelly have crafted a world-class dining (and drinking) destination that rivals L.A.’s more established fine dining institutions.
